Section A

  1. The greatest number which divides both 83 and 138, leaving remainders 5 and 8 respectively, is :
    (a) 13
    (b) 65
    (c) 26
    (d) 39

  2. If α and β are the zeroes of the polynomial $p(x) = 2x² − 7x + 3$, then the value of $α^2 + β^2$ is :
    (a) 10
    (b) $\frac{37}{4}$
    (c) $\frac{3}{2}$
    (d) 37

  3. If the sum of the zeroes of the quadratic polynomial $p(x) = kx² + 2x + 3k$ is equal to the product of its zeroes, then the value of k is :
    (a) $3/2$
    (b) $−3/2$
    (c) $2/3$
    (d) $−2/3$

  4. The pair of equations x = a and y = b represent the lines which are :
    (a) parallel
    (b) intersecting at (b, a)
    (c) coincident
    (d) intersecting at (a, b)

  5. If one equation of a pair of dependent equations is $3x + 5y = 4$, then the second equation can be :
    (a) $6x + 10y = 8$
    (b) $9x − 15y + 12 = 0$
    (c) $-9x + 15y = -12$
    (d) $-6x − 10y = 8$

  6. If one root of the equation $2x² − 5x + (λ-4) = 0$ is the reciprocal of the other, then the value of $λ$ is :
    (a) 5
    (b) 4
    (c) 6
    (d) 8

  7. The 4th term from the end of an AP $-11, -8, -5, … , −49$
    is :
    (b) 37
    (a) 40
    (c) 43
    (d) 58

  8. The perimeters of two similar triangles are 42 cm and 35 cm respectively.
    If one side of the first triangle is 12 cm, then the corresponding side of the second triangle is :
    (a) 5 cm
    (b) 7.5 cm
    (c) 8 cm
    (d) 10 cm

  9. E and F are points on the sides $PQ$ and $PR$ respectively of $△PQR$.
    If $EF ∥ QR$ and $PE = 4$ cm, $QE =$ 3 cm and $EF = 4$ cm, then the length of $QR$ is :
    (a) 3 cm
    (b) 4 cm
    (c) 7 cm
    (d) 6 cm

  10. If two tangents inclined at an angle of 60° are drawn to a circle of radius 5 cm from an external point, then the length of each tangent is equal to :
    (a) $5\sqrt 3$ cm
    (b) 10 cm
    (c) $\dfrac{5}{\sqrt{3}}$ cm
    (d) $\dfrac{5\sqrt 3}{2}$ cm

  11. If $\tan A = \dfrac{3}{4}$, then the value of $\dfrac{4 \sin A − 2 \cos A}{4 \sin A + 2 \cos A}$
    is :
    (a) 5
    (b) $1/5$
    (c) 6
    (d) $1/6$

  12. If $2 \sin 2A = √3$, then A is equal to :
    (a) 60°
    (b) 45°
    (c) 90°
    (d) 30°

  13. It is found that on walking 20 m towards a chimney in a horizontal line through its base, the elevation of its top changes from 30° to 60°.
    The height of the chimney is :
    (a) $20\sqrt{3}$ m
    (b) $10\sqrt{3}$ m
    (c) $\dfrac{20\sqrt{3}}{2}$ m
    (d) $\dfrac{20}{\sqrt{3}}$ m

  14. The area of a sector of a circle of radius 16 cm cut off by an arc of length 18.5 cm is :
    (a) 168 cm²
    (b) 148 cm²
    (c) 154 cm²
    (d) 176 cm²

  15. A hemispherical bowl is made of steel of thickness 1 cm.
    The inner radius of the bowl is 5 cm.
    The volume of steel used (in cm³) is :
    (a) 182
    (b) $\dfrac{182}{3}\pi$
    (c) $\dfrac{682}{3}\pi$
    (d) $\dfrac{364}{3}\pi$

  16. The mean and median of a frequency distribution are 43 and 43.4 respectively.
    The mode is :
    (a) 43.25
    (b) 42.25
    (c) 44.2
    (d) 49.5

  17. The probability that a leap year selected at random will contain 53 Sundays and 53 Mondays is :
    (a) $\dfrac{1}{7}$
    (b) $\dfrac{2}{7}$
    (c) $\dfrac{3}{7}$
    (d) $\dfrac{4}{7}$

  18. The probability for a randomly selected number out of ${1, 2, 3, 4, … , 25}$ to be a prime number is :
    (a) $\dfrac{8}{25}$
    (b) $\dfrac{10}{25}$
    (c) $\dfrac{11}{25}$
    (d) $\dfrac{9}{25}$

  19. Assertion (A): The tangents drawn at the end points of a diameter of a circle are parallel to each other.
    Reason (R): The tangent to a circle is perpendicular to the radius at the point of contact.

  20. Assertion (A): The surface area of the cuboid formed by joining two cubes of side 4 cm each end to end is 160 cm².
    Reason (R): Surface area of a cuboid of dimensions $l, b$ and $h$ is $2(lb + bh + hl)$.
